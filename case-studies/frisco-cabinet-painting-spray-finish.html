Cabinet Painting - Frisco, TX

Frisco cabinet painting with spray finish planning, cure time, and hardware detail.

A Frisco cabinet painting case study covering cleaning, sanding, bonding primer, spray finish, cure time, hardware changes, and kitchen protection.

CityFrisco, TX
Timing1-2 week finish window
Budget lensCabinet refinish planning
The issue

What made this scope worth planning carefully.

Cabinet painting fails when it is treated like wall painting. Grease, slick factory finish, door handling, old hinges, and cure time decide whether the finish survives daily use.

The plan

How ELF would sequence the work.

Remove doors, label hardware, degrease, sand, prime with a bonding system, spray doors in a controlled setup, and brush or roll boxes only where the finish plan supports it.

The finish

The standard that makes the project feel complete.

A smooth enamel finish, adjusted doors, updated pulls, and careful cure instructions make the kitchen feel renewed without replacing boxes that still work.

Scope checklist

  • Door and box condition check
  • Degrease and sand workflow
  • Bonding primer
  • Sprayed door finish
  • Hardware and adjustment pass

Quote signals

  • Do not paint peeling thermofoil
  • Allow real cure time
  • Update pulls after hole spacing is measured
  • Plan dust and kitchen access
Outcome

The customer can compare painting versus replacement clearly because prep labor, finish durability, door condition, and hardware changes are separated in the scope.
