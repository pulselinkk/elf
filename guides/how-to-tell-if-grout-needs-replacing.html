Start with the real decision.

Dirty grout, cracked grout, missing grout, and wet grout point to different causes. This guide helps you decide whether maintenance is enough or whether the tile system needs a more serious repair.

Grout is a visible joint, but it also helps protect the tile assembly from water and debris. When grout starts failing in a shower, the important question is whether the failure is only at the surface or whether water has already moved behind tile.

In kitchens and floors, grout failure is often about movement, heavy use, cleaning chemicals, or poor original installation. The repair should match the cause, not just the color of the joint.

North Texas notes.

DFW homes have patterns shaped by heat, storms, clay soil, slab construction, hard water, and buyer expectations.

Local planning notes
  • Hard water in many North Texas homes leaves mineral deposits that can look like grout failure.
  • Bathroom fans that vent poorly make grout and caulk age faster.
  • Slab movement can telegraph through tile floors and open grout joints.
Slow down when needed

Active water, unsafe access, structural symptoms, electrical risk, gas, refrigerant, or hidden waterproofing should be addressed before cosmetic work.

Homeowner checklist.

Use this before buying materials, starting DIY work, or asking for an estimate.

Checklist
  • Press gently around suspect tile to check for movement.
  • Look for dark grout that stays wet longer than nearby joints.
  • Check corners and tub lines for failed caulk.
  • Confirm the bath fan pulls air before blaming grout alone.
  • Use matching grout type and color for visible repairs.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers for deciding whether this is DIY, maintenance, repair, or a professional project.

Can stained grout be saved?

Often yes if the grout is solid and the issue is surface staining or hard-water residue.

Should shower corners be grout or caulk?

Changes of plane usually perform better with flexible sealant because walls and tubs move differently.

Is cracked grout always water damage?

No, but recurring cracks in a wet area deserve a closer look before cosmetic repair.

Can ELF regrout a shower?

Yes, and we can also identify when a shower needs waterproofing or tile repair beyond grout.
