Start with the real decision.

Flat, eggshell, satin, and semi-gloss finishes all behave differently. The right finish depends on traffic, wall texture, light, cleaning needs, and whether imperfections should be hidden or tolerated.

Paint finish affects how a wall looks in daylight, how easily it cleans, and how much it reveals drywall imperfections. Higher sheen is usually more washable, but it also shows surface flaws.

In DFW homes, builder flat paint often marks easily. A planned repaint should match sheen to the room instead of using one finish everywhere.

Field note: A finish should pass three tests: close-up detail, room-wide balance, and how it looks in ordinary daylight.

North Texas notes.

DFW homes have patterns shaped by heat, storms, clay soil, slab construction, hard water, and buyer expectations.

Local planning notes
  • Strong Texas sunlight can make sheen differences obvious across tall walls.
  • Builder flat paint in newer homes often needs upgrade in halls and family areas.
  • Bathrooms need ventilation and bath-rated paint, not sheen alone.
Slow down when needed

Active water, unsafe access, structural symptoms, electrical risk, gas, refrigerant, or hidden waterproofing should be addressed before cosmetic work.

Homeowner checklist.

Use this before buying materials, starting DIY work, or asking for an estimate.

Checklist
  • View paint samples in morning and evening light.
  • Use consistent sheen across connected open rooms.
  • Do not use wall paint on cabinets.
  • Prime patches to avoid flashing.
  • Let paint cure before aggressive cleaning.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers for deciding whether this is DIY, maintenance, repair, or a professional project.

What is the best finish for interior walls?

Eggshell is a strong default for many living areas because it balances appearance and cleanability.

Should trim be semi-gloss?

Usually yes, because trim and doors take more contact and cleaning.

Why do patched areas show through paint?

Primer, texture, sheen, or paint age may not match the surrounding surface.

Is satin too shiny for walls?

Not in high-use rooms, but it can reveal imperfections more than eggshell.
