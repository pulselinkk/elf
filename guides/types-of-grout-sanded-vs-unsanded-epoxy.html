Start with the real decision.

Grout choice affects durability, cleaning, installation difficulty, and how finished tile looks. Sanded, unsanded, and epoxy grout each have a place.

The tile usually gets the attention, but grout can make or break the finished project. Wrong grout type can scratch tile, crack in wide joints, stain early, or look uneven.

Before choosing grout, confirm tile material, joint size, surface texture, room use, and whether the area is wet, dry, vertical, or on the floor.

Field note: If the same issue has returned more than once, solve the cause before spending money on the finish.

North Texas notes.

DFW homes have patterns shaped by heat, storms, clay soil, slab construction, hard water, and buyer expectations.

Local planning notes
  • Hard water can show quickly on dark grout in North Texas showers.
  • High-traffic family floors need grout that can handle cleaning without breaking down.
  • Shower grout does not replace waterproofing behind tile.
Slow down when needed

Active water, unsafe access, structural symptoms, electrical risk, gas, refrigerant, or hidden waterproofing should be addressed before cosmetic work.

Homeowner checklist.

Use this before buying materials, starting DIY work, or asking for an estimate.

Checklist
  • Do not use sanded grout on easily scratched tile without checking.
  • Avoid extreme contrast unless the tile layout is very clean.
  • Read cure time before shower use.
  • Use matching caulk at movement joints.
  • Keep leftover grout color information for repairs.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers for deciding whether this is DIY, maintenance, repair, or a professional project.

Is epoxy grout worth it?

It can be worth it for stain resistance, but it costs more and requires careful installation.

Is sanded grout always stronger?

It is better for many wider joints, but it is not right for every tile surface.

Does grout make a shower waterproof?

No. Waterproofing must be behind the tile. Grout is not the waterproof layer.

Why does grout color look uneven?

Water ratio, cleaning, curing, tile absorption, and lighting can all affect perceived color.
