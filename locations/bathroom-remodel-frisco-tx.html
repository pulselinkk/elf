What matters most in Frisco.

A Frisco bathroom remodel should start with use, not tile samples. Many homes already have enough square footage, but the shower, vanity, fan, mirror, and storage details do not match how the household lives now.

ELF plans the room as a wet system and a daily routine: waterproofing, drainage, lighting, fan performance, easy-clean surfaces, and practical storage before decorative upgrades.

Before you book.

A practical checklist for homeowners who want a cleaner estimate and fewer surprises.

Quick prep: send clear photos, rough measurements, the city, timeline, and what bothers you most. That turns the first conversation into a real scope discussion.
  • Measure vanity, shower, and ceiling height before shopping.
  • Decide if the tub matters before designing the shower.
  • Choose grout with cleaning habits in mind.
  • Upgrade the fan if humidity lingers.
  • Confirm glass swing and towel locations before tile is finished.

Local FAQs.

Short answers to common questions before scheduling bathroom remodel work in Frisco.

How much does a Frisco bathroom remodel cost?

Focused updates can be in the high four figures, while shower rebuilds and primary suite remodels commonly range much higher depending on tile, vanity, plumbing, and glass.

Can I update just the shower?

Yes. Shower-only remodels work well when the rest of the bath is in good condition, but transitions need planning so the room feels intentional.

Is frameless glass worth it?

Often, in a primary bath. It raises the finish level, but it also needs good layout, tile edges, and maintenance habits.
