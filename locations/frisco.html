Local context

The fastest-growing city in DFW.

Frisco's population has roughly tripled since 2010 — most of the housing stock is post-2005, with significant new construction stretching west toward Hwy 380 and Phillips Creek Ranch. That young housing means homeowners aren't typically dealing with 1970s wiring or asbestos popcorn ceilings, but they ARE seeing settlement cracks in 5-15 year-old slabs as the Blackland Prairie clay finally cycles through wet/dry seasons.

The most common Frisco call we get: cosmetic upgrades to original-build interiors — repaint builder-grade walls, replace builder-grade carpet with LVP, refinish honey-oak cabinets, swap out cheap fixtures for higher-end. These projects typically run $8,000–$35,000 and dramatically increase resale value in Frisco's hot market.

Frisco-specific drywall pattern

Most Frisco homes built 2005-2018 have orange peel texture sprayed at builder pace. We see consistent seasonal cracks at the same locations: above interior doorways, at corners where 9'+ ceilings meet walls, and at the joint between kitchen and living areas where structural beams transition. We patch with mesh tape and flexible compound so they don't recur.

Top services in Frisco

What we get called for here.

Kitchen remodels are our #1 Frisco service. Most are mid-range upgrades ($25,000–$55,000) — semi-custom cabinets, quartz counters, herringbone backsplashes, premium appliances. We've done several in Stonebriar, The Trails, and Newman Village.

Bathroom remodels are #2. Frisco master bathrooms are typically larger than DFW average (200+ sqft), which means more tile and more plumbing. Curbless walk-in showers with linear drains are popular here — we do 2-3 per month in Frisco alone.

Whole-house exterior painting — Frisco HOAs require maintenance, and 10+ year-old paint shows badly under Texas UV. We use Sherwin-Williams Duration or Emerald Exterior to get 8-10 year cycles between repaints.

LVP flooring installation — replacing original builder carpet with premium 20-mil LVP. Average Frisco install: 1,800–2,400 sqft, $14,000–$22,000.
