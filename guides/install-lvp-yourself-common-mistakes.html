North Texas notes.

DFW homes have specific repair and design patterns because of heat, clay soil, storms, hard water, humidity, slabs, and resale expectations.

Local planning notes
  • DFW slab homes often need leveling before floating floors.
  • Large open rooms show plank direction and low spots.
  • Rental turnovers need durable products with available replacement stock.
Slow down when needed

Active water, structural symptoms, unsafe access, electrical risk, or hidden waterproofing should be solved before cosmetic finish work.

Homeowner checklist.

Use this before starting the project or asking for an estimate.

Checklist
  • Acclimate if product requires it.
  • Use a straightedge to find low spots.
  • Plan first and last row width.
  • Do not pinch planks under trim.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers for deciding whether this is DIY, maintenance, repair, or a professional project.

Can I install LVP myself?

Yes in simple rooms with flat floors and clear instructions. Whole-house installs are more demanding.

Why is my LVP separating?

Flatness, locking damage, expansion gaps, product quality, or installation method may be involved.

Can LVP go over tile?

Sometimes, if tile is flat, bonded, and transitions work.

Do I need underlayment?

Only if the product requires or allows it. Wrong underlayment can cause problems.
