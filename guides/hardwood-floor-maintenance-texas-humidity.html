This guide is about protecting existing hardwood through humidity swings.

Texas hardwood maintenance focuses on moisture movement, cleaning habits, HVAC consistency, felt protection, finish wear, and when seasonal gaps are normal.

A homeowner maintaining hardwood is trying to prevent damage, not choose a new floor. The practical questions are about indoor humidity, sunlight, entry mats, chair pads, pet nails, mop water, cleaning products, and how to spot finish wear before bare wood is exposed.

North Texas homes can swing from humid storms to dry conditioned air quickly. The goal is stable living conditions and gentle cleaning, not aggressive wet mopping or panic over every seasonal gap.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers for deciding whether this is DIY, maintenance, repair, or a professional project.

Why are gaps appearing in my hardwood?

Seasonal humidity changes can cause wood to shrink and expand.

Can cupped floors be sanded flat?

Not until the moisture source is corrected and the floor stabilizes.

What cleaner should I use?

Use a cleaner compatible with the floor finish and avoid steam or excess water.

Is engineered hardwood easier in Texas?

Often, especially on slabs, but it still needs moisture care.
