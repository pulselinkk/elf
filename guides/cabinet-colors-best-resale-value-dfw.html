North Texas notes.

DFW homes have specific repair and design patterns because of heat, clay soil, storms, hard water, humidity, slabs, and resale expectations.

Local planning notes
  • Plano and McKinney older kitchens often benefit from warm neutral cabinet paint.
  • Frisco and Allen family homes need colors that hide daily wear better than pure white.
  • Southlake premium kitchens can support richer island colors when balanced.
Slow down when needed

Active water, structural symptoms, unsafe access, electrical risk, or hidden waterproofing should be solved before cosmetic finish work.

Homeowner checklist.

Use this before starting the project or asking for an estimate.

Checklist
  • Paint large sample boards.
  • View colors morning and evening.
  • Do not choose cabinet color without counter sample.
  • Use durable finish, not wall paint.
  • Keep bold colors easy to repaint if resale matters.
Estimate tip

Send context, not only close-ups.

Include one wide photo, one close-up, rough measurements, the city, when the issue started, and what has already been tried.

Decision rule

Protect the expensive layer.

When water, substrate, structure, or finish quality is involved, solve that layer before spending on visible finishes.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers for deciding whether this is DIY, maintenance, repair, or a professional project.

What cabinet color is best for resale in DFW?

Warm white, soft greige, and balanced taupe are usually safe.

Are gray cabinets outdated?

Cool gray is fading, but warmer gray-beige tones still work.

Can I use color on an island?

Yes. Islands are a good place for muted green, navy, or wood tone.

Does cabinet paint hold up?

It can when prep, primer, product, and cure time are handled correctly.
