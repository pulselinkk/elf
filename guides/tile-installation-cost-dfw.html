Tile is finish work and construction at the same time.

Decorative work is usually less complex than floors and showers. Wet areas need waterproofing and more careful sequencing.

A backsplash is mainly precision finish work. A shower is a waterproofing system with tile on top. A floor is only as good as its flatness, substrate, and movement control.

That is why tile prices vary so much in DFW. The visible material is only one piece of the job. Layout planning, substrate repair, membranes, grout, edge trim, cuts, and cleanup all affect the finished result.

This guide separates decorative tile from wet-area tile so the estimate matches the real risk.

Field note: Best budget move: pick a simple layout and spend on prep. A basic tile installed beautifully usually beats an expensive tile installed over a poor surface.

What moves the price.

Tile estimates should explain the surface, not just the square footage.

Substrate

Flatness determines the final look.

Walls and floors need correction before tile. Tile does not hide waves; it highlights them.

Prep first
Pattern

Pattern changes labor.

Straight lay is faster. Herringbone, diagonal, mosaics, and border work require more layout time and cuts.

Beauty has math
Waterproofing

Wet areas need systems.

Showers need a connected approach at pan, walls, niches, benches, and valve openings.

Not optional
Material

Stone and porcelain behave differently.

Natural stone may need sealing and careful handling. Porcelain is durable but can be harder to cut.

Pick for the room
Grout

Grout is a performance choice.

Sanded, unsanded, high-performance cement, and epoxy grout each suit different gaps and maintenance needs.

Not just color
Edges

Open ends need a clean finish.

Schluter trim, bullnose, mitered edges, or return details should be decided before layout starts.

Avoid awkward endings

DFW tile notes.

Local conditions, buyer expectations, climate, and typical construction methods change how a project should be planned in North Texas.

Local planning notes
  • Hard water makes shower glass, grout, and dark tile show mineral marks faster. Maintenance should influence product choices.
  • Slab movement means floor tile may need crack isolation or careful joint planning in some homes.
  • Builder backsplashes often hide uneven drywall. A small wall can still need meaningful prep.
  • Large-format shower tile looks modern, but the walls must be very flat for it to look premium.
Before you approve a quote

Ask whether the proposal includes protection, material handling, disposal, prep, finish details, and cleanup. The missing line items are usually where budgets get messy.

Good rule

If two quotes are far apart, compare scope line by line before assuming one contractor is simply cheaper.

How the work should unfold.

A clean sequence protects the home, reduces rework, and keeps decisions from arriving too late.

Surface and layout review

Check substrate, tile size, pattern, transitions, edge endings, and wet-area requirements.

Demo and prep

Remove old finish, repair surface, flatten where needed, install backer or membrane if required.

Set tile

Dry layout, cut, set, control lippage, clean joints, and let materials cure properly.

Grout and finish

Grout, seal if needed, caulk changes of plane, reset fixtures, and clean haze.

Tile planning checklist.

Use this before requesting a quote or buying materials. It keeps the conversation concrete.

Checklist
  • Order 10-15 percent extra tile for cuts and future repairs.
  • Decide edge trim before tile day.
  • Confirm grout type and color with the actual tile.
  • Ask how waterproofing will be handled in showers.
  • Check tile lot numbers for color consistency.
  • Plan outlet cover depth on backsplashes.
Do not skip
  • Do not set shower tile without a verified waterproofing plan.
  • Do not assume large tile means faster installation.
  • Do not ignore cracked or hollow existing tile before installing new flooring.
  • Do not choose white grout in heavy-use areas without accepting maintenance.
Estimate tip

Ask for the finish standard.

Every quote should define what counts as complete: cleanup, touch-ups, paint blending, material disposal, and what happens if hidden damage appears.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers to the questions homeowners ask before they commit to the scope.

How much does backsplash tile cost in DFW?

Most kitchen backsplashes land between $900 and $3,500 depending on size, tile type, pattern, outlets, prep, and grout.

Why does shower tile cost more than floor tile?

A shower is a water management system. Waterproofing, slope, niches, valves, corners, and glass readiness all add labor and risk.

Is epoxy grout worth it?

It can be worth it in showers, backsplashes, and high-stain areas. It costs more and requires careful installation, but maintenance is usually easier.

Can tile be installed over tile?

Sometimes, but only if the existing tile is sound, flat, and appropriate for the product. Many jobs are better with demo and fresh prep.
