Where fence DIY usually stops being simple.

Fence repair is outdoors, load-bearing, property-line sensitive, and affected by wind. This section separates board-level fixes from post, gate, root, and utility issues that need a different plan.

DIY boundary

Board repair

Replacing a cracked picket, adding missing screws, or touching up stain is usually a controlled repair if the rails and post are still solid.

DIY boundary

Gate reality

A sagging gate is rarely solved by the latch alone. Check hinge screws, frame square, post movement, latch alignment, and whether the gate is too heavy for the post.

DIY boundary

Digging risk

Posts introduce utility marking, concrete removal, soil conditions, neighbor access, root conflict, and straight-line layout. That is the point where DIY risk jumps.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers for deciding whether this is DIY, maintenance, repair, or a professional project.

Can I repair a fence myself?

Yes for many picket and hardware repairs. Posts, gates, and long sections are more involved.

Why does my gate keep sagging?

The post, hinge, or frame may be moving. A latch alone will not solve it.

Should I brace a leaning fence?

Temporary bracing can help, but failed posts usually need reset or replacement.

Can roots damage fences?

Yes. Roots can move posts, panels, and concrete footings.
