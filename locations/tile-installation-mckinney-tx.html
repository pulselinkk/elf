Texas heat and tile

Why tile fails prematurely here.

McKinney's climate puts unusual stress on tile installations. Summer temperatures cause slab expansion — if tile was installed without proper expansion joints or using the wrong thinset, you'll get cracking and tenting (tiles popping up) within 3-5 years. We see this constantly on DIY installs and cheap contractor work.

The fix is proper technique: large-format tile (12x24 and larger) requires back-buttering in addition to floor coverage, minimum 95% thinset coverage on exterior and wet applications, and expansion joints at every 8-10 feet and at all perimeter walls. We don't skip these steps to save time.

Backsplash installation

McKinney's most popular tile upgrade.

The kitchen backsplash is the single highest-ROI tile project in McKinney. A well-executed backsplash transforms an entire kitchen's feel for $1,500-$3,500 — far less than cabinet replacement. Most popular in McKinney right now: 3x12 subway in stacked vertical pattern (white or warm gray), 4x12 zellige-style ceramic with variation, and marble herringbone mosaic behind the range.

For backsplashes, we always: remove the outlet covers and replace them after tiling (never tile around them), extend the tile to the underside of the cabinets for a clean line, and use unsanded grout in colors that complement the stone. Grouting the day after setting allows full cure and prevents cracking.

Shower tile in McKinney

What a proper shower retile looks like.

A shower tile job done right involves: full demo of existing tile and substrate, inspection of framing for moisture damage (we find it in about 30% of McKinney shower retiles), installation of Schluter KERDI or USG Durock waterproofing system, setting the tile in modified thinset, and grouting with epoxy or polymer-modified grout.

We don't tile over existing tile. It adds thickness, can cause door clearance issues, and if there's any moisture behind the old tile (there usually is), you're sealing the problem in. Full demo takes one day and saves years of callback issues.
