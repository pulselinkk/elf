Denton's unique housing stock

Why Denton paint jobs are different.

Denton has some of the most varied housing stock in North Texas. Within the city limits you'll find: 1910-1940s Craftsman and Foursquare bungalows near the historic Square (requires flexible masking and careful prep on original woodwork), 1950s-70s ranch homes in the University neighborhoods (often the first paint job in decades), 1980s-90s brick with minimal wood trim, 2000s suburban in newer areas like Robson Ranch, and active new construction throughout the Rayzor Ranch corridor.

Each of these requires different prep, primer choices, and application methods. On historic homes near the Square, we take extra care with original moldings and use oil-based primer on any bare wood before topcoating. Robson Ranch (55+ active adult community) specifically requests low-VOC paints — we stock them as standard.

Denton-specific challenges

What affects paint jobs here.

Heat and UV: Denton averages 229 sunny days/year and summer temps regularly hit 105°F. Exterior paint needs to be rated for Texas UV — we use Sherwin-Williams Emerald Exterior or Duration as our standard (both have lifetime warranties). We don't use budget exterior paint on Denton homes; the UV degradation within 2 years makes it false economy.

Cedar siding: Many Denton ranch homes have cedar or T1-11 siding that bleeds tannins through paint. We always prime with a shellac-based primer (Zinsser BIN) on bare cedar before topcoating. Skipping this step is why you see brown bleed-through on DIY repaints.

High humidity + heavy AC use: Denton's humidity swings cause wood expansion/contraction. We caulk all seams with paintable elastomeric caulk that moves with the wood, not rigid acrylic that cracks.
