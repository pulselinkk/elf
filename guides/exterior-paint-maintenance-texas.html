North Texas notes.

DFW homes have specific repair and design patterns because of heat, clay soil, storms, hard water, humidity, slabs, and resale expectations.

Local planning notes
  • DFW west-facing doors and trim fail fast in summer.
  • HOA communities may control color changes.

Homeowner checklist.

Use this before starting the project or asking for an estimate.

Checklist
  • Inspect after spring storms.
  • Touch caulk before it opens wide.
  • Do not paint soft trim.
  • Use primer on bare substrate.
  • Keep paint color and sheen records.
Estimate tip

Send context, not only close-ups.

Include one wide photo, one close-up, rough measurements, the city, when the issue started, and what has already been tried.

Decision rule

Protect the expensive layer.

When water, substrate, structure, or finish quality is involved, solve that layer before spending on visible finishes.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers for deciding whether this is DIY, maintenance, repair, or a professional project.

How often should exterior paint be checked?

At least yearly, and after major storms.

Can I touch up exterior paint?

Small areas can be touched up, but fading may make color mismatch visible.

Why does paint peel at trim ends?

Water often enters end grain or failed joints.

Should I repaint before selling?

If exterior paint is peeling, faded, or chalky, strategic repainting can improve buyer confidence.
