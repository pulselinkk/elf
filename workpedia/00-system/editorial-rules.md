# Editorial Rules

## Voice

ELF content should sound practical, calm, and expert. It should not sound like a generic marketing agency. Use plain language, but include enough detail that a homeowner learns something real.

## Page Standard

Every serious page should include:

- A clear homeowner decision.
- A local or service-specific insight.
- A risk or hidden failure mode.
- A preparation checklist.
- A quote input list.
- A next step that fits the scope.

## Avoid

- Empty claims like "high quality service".
- Repeating the city name without adding city-specific knowledge.
- Saying "affordable" without explaining what controls price.
- Publishing exact permit, code, or legal guidance without verification.
- Writing long paragraphs that cannot be scanned.

## Preferred Blocks

- "Best first message"
- "What changes the price"
- "What can go wrong"
- "Before we arrive"
- "Good / better / best"
- "Repair vs replace"
- "DIY safe / call a pro"
- "Photos to send"
- "Local note"
