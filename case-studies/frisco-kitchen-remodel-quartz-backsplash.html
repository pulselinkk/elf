Kitchen Remodel - Frisco, TX

Frisco kitchen remodel with quartz, backsplash, and cleaner cabinet flow.

A practical Frisco kitchen remodel case study covering layout decisions, quartz counters, backsplash detail, cabinet planning, lighting, and disruption control.

CityFrisco, TX
Timing5-8 week planning window
Budget lensMid-range remodel planning
The issue

What made this scope worth planning carefully.

Frisco kitchens often have good square footage but builder-grade finishes, shallow storage, uneven lighting, and a backsplash/counter combination that dates the room before the cabinets actually fail.

The plan

How ELF would sequence the work.

The best path is to preserve what still works, upgrade the finish layer, confirm appliance openings early, and sequence cabinets, counters, tile, and paint so the kitchen is not opened twice.

The finish

The standard that makes the project feel complete.

Quartz with subtle movement, a clean backsplash layout, cabinet hardware with a warm metal finish, under-cabinet lighting, and durable paint give the kitchen a higher-end look without forcing a full layout reset.

Scope checklist

  • Cabinet assessment and finish plan
  • Countertop template coordination
  • Backsplash surface prep and layout
  • Lighting and outlet coordination
  • Final punch list and paint touch-ups

Quote signals

  • Keep plumbing in place when possible
  • Measure appliance clearances before ordering
  • Choose backsplash layout before counters are templated
  • Plan a temporary kitchen if work lasts more than a week

Outcome

The project becomes easier to price because finish level, material allowances, demolition needs, and installation order are clear before the first cabinet door comes off.
