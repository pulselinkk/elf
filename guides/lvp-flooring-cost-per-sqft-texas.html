A cheap plank can become expensive if the slab is not ready.

Ranges are per square foot unless noted. Stairs, complex patterns, old tile demo, and subfloor correction can change the total.

LVP is popular in Texas because it handles busy homes, pets, and everyday spills better than many natural materials. But the installation still depends on the surface underneath.

DFW slab homes often need leveling, crack review, moisture checks, or transition planning before planks go down. Skipping that prep can lead to clicking, gaps, bounce, and visible waves across rooms.

This guide breaks the price into material, labor, demolition, prep, trim, and special areas so the final number is easier to understand.

Field note: Best budget move: buy a better mid-range plank and invest in prep. A durable wear layer over a flat floor usually outperforms a premium color installed over a bad slab.

What moves the price.

Flooring quotes should explain what happens before the planks are installed.

Wear layer

The wear layer protects the look.

For busy family homes, a stronger wear layer is usually worth the upgrade. Thin bargain floors can show traffic faster.

12-20 mil is common
Flatness

Flat floors are quieter floors.

LVP locking systems need the slab within tolerance. Low spots, ridges, and old mortar can cause movement and noise.

Prep prevents callbacks
Moisture

Slab moisture should be respected.

Texas slabs can transmit moisture. Product specs and site conditions should match before installation.

Check before covering
Transitions

Doorways and room changes need design.

A good install plans transitions at tile, carpet, exterior doors, fireplaces, and stair edges.

Texas LVP notes.

Local conditions, buyer expectations, climate, and typical construction methods change how a project should be planned in North Texas.

Local planning notes
  • Slab movement in North Texas makes expansion gaps and transition planning important. Tight installs can fail when the house moves seasonally.
  • Old ceramic tile demo is one of the biggest budget surprises. It is slower, dustier, and more prep-heavy than carpet removal.
  • Dark floors show dust faster in Texas sunlight. Mid-tone oak and warm natural colors are often easier to live with.
  • Large open floor plans need careful plank direction. The longest visual run usually determines the cleanest layout.
Before you approve a quote

Ask whether the proposal includes protection, material handling, disposal, prep, finish details, and cleanup. The missing line items are usually where budgets get messy.

Good rule

If two quotes are far apart, compare scope line by line before assuming one contractor is simply cheaper.

How the work should unfold.

A clean sequence protects the home, reduces rework, and keeps decisions from arriving too late.

Measure and inspect

Measure rooms, identify transitions, check existing floor type, review slab, and plan furniture movement.

Material and layout approval

Select plank, trim strategy, stair parts, transition colors, and installation direction.

Demo and prep

Remove old flooring, clean slab, correct high and low spots, address moisture concerns, and stage materials.

Install and finish trim

Install planks, transitions, baseboards or shoe molding, caulk, paint touch-ups, and final cleanup.

LVP planning checklist.

Use this before requesting a quote or buying materials. It keeps the conversation concrete.

Checklist
  • Ask for material, labor, demo, prep, and trim as separate lines.
  • Confirm the wear layer and warranty before buying the plank.
  • Check whether old tile removal is included.
  • Decide between baseboard removal and shoe molding.
  • Plan where furniture will move during installation.
  • Keep extra cartons for future plank replacement.
Do not skip
  • Do not install over a slab outside manufacturer flatness tolerance.
  • Do not ignore moisture guidance for the specific plank.
  • Do not mix random transition pieces that clash with the floor.
  • Do not assume stairs are included in a per-square-foot quote.
Estimate tip

Ask for the finish standard.

Every quote should define what counts as complete: cleanup, touch-ups, paint blending, material disposal, and what happens if hidden damage appears.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers to the questions homeowners ask before they commit to the scope.

What is a realistic all-in LVP cost in Texas?

Many professional installs land between $7 and $14 per square foot all-in when material, labor, normal prep, and trim are included. Heavy demo or leveling can raise that.

Is LVP good for Texas homes?

Yes, when installed over a properly prepared surface and selected for the traffic level. It handles pets, spills, and everyday family use well.

Can LVP go over tile?

Sometimes, but the tile must be flat, stable, and within product guidelines. Many older tile floors need demo or leveling first.

What is better: shoe molding or removing baseboards?

Removing and reinstalling baseboards usually looks cleaner. Shoe molding can be more budget-friendly and less disruptive. The right choice depends on the home and finish goal.
