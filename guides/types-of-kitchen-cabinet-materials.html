Start with the real decision.

Cabinet doors may look similar from across the room, but material affects durability, paint quality, water resistance, repair options, and whether a cabinet is worth saving.

Kitchen cabinet planning should start with the existing boxes. If boxes are square, dry, and functional, refinishing can be a strong value. If boxes are swollen, sagging, or poorly laid out, new doors and paint will not fix the real problem.

DFW homes from different build eras have very different cabinet quality. Some older boxes are worth saving; some builder-grade products are better replaced when function matters.

Field note: If the same issue has returned more than once, solve the cause before spending money on the finish.

North Texas notes.

DFW homes have patterns shaped by heat, storms, clay soil, slab construction, hard water, and buyer expectations.

Local planning notes
  • Plano and Frisco homes often have cabinets that can be refinished if layout still works.
  • Sink bases should be inspected for old leaks before painting.
  • Resale-safe cabinet colors can matter more than exotic material upgrades.
Slow down when needed

Active water, unsafe access, structural symptoms, electrical risk, gas, refrigerant, or hidden waterproofing should be addressed before cosmetic work.

Homeowner checklist.

Use this before buying materials, starting DIY work, or asking for an estimate.

Checklist
  • Look under the sink for swelling or staining.
  • Check whether doors close square.
  • Identify thermofoil before promising paint.
  • Test cabinet sides and end panels for damage.
  • Budget for hardware if hole spacing changes.
Estimate tip

Send context, not only close-ups.

Include one wide photo, one close-up, rough measurements, the city, when the issue started, and what has already been tried.

Decision rule

Protect the expensive layer.

When water, structure, substrate, code, or finish quality is involved, solve that layer before spending on visible finishes.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers for deciding whether this is DIY, maintenance, repair, or a professional project.

Are MDF cabinets bad?

No. MDF can be excellent for painted doors when dry and well made, but it does not like water damage.

Is plywood better than particleboard?

Often yes for durability and screw holding, especially in boxes.

Can thermofoil cabinets be painted?

Sometimes, but peeling thermofoil can make refinishing unreliable.

When should cabinets be replaced?

Replace when layout, structure, water damage, or function is the real problem.
