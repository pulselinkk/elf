# Answer Bank - Volume 01

## Quote Prompts

- Send one wide photo, one closeup, rough dimensions, city, and ideal timing.
- If water was involved, confirm whether the source is fixed before asking for finish repair.
- For remodels, include must-haves, nice-to-haves, and the budget range you are comfortable exploring.
- For local pages, include neighborhood, HOA constraints, access limits, and deadline.

## Trust Blocks

- Flat pricing works best when scope is written clearly before work starts.
- Good prep is not decoration. It is the part of the job that makes the visible finish last.
- The cheapest quote often removes the invisible work: protection, prep, sequencing, and cleanup.
- A clean finish depends on the surface behind it.

## Local Blocks

- DFW homes vary by era, and the repair should match the era of the house.
- North Texas heat, slab movement, humidity swings, and storms all change how home repairs should be scoped.
- In HOA-visible neighborhoods, exterior details need to look intentional from the street.
- Older homes need more diagnostic patience before closing up walls, ceilings, or trim.

## CTA Variants

- Start with photos and we will help sort the scope.
- Send the room, city, photos, and timeline for a cleaner estimate.
- Build the first scope before you spend the big money.
- Tell us what must be fixed, what can wait, and what result you want.
