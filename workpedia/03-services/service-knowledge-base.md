# Service Knowledge Base - Volume 01

## Painting

Key homeowner decisions:

- Interior or exterior.
- Walls only, walls and ceilings, or full trim package.
- Refresh, color change, stain blocking, or resale neutralization.
- Matte, eggshell, satin, semi-gloss, or enamel finish.
- Roll/brush finish versus spray finish for cabinets and trim.

What changes price:

- Wall height.
- Number of colors.
- Trim complexity.
- Repairs before paint.
- Furniture and protection.
- Primer requirements.
- Exterior elevation exposure.
- Siding material.

Common failures:

- Painting over chalky exterior surfaces.
- Skipping primer on bare wood or stains.
- Using flat paint in high-touch areas.
- Not caulking moving joints with flexible caulk.
- Rolling cabinet doors instead of using a proper enamel process.

Quote inputs:

- Wide photos of every room or elevation.
- Closeups of peeling, stains, cracks, and trim.
- Desired color and sheen if known.
- Whether the home is occupied, staged, vacant, or rental.

## Drywall

Key homeowner decisions:

- Patch only or paint the whole wall.
- Match orange peel, knockdown, skip trowel, smooth, or popcorn.
- Repair visible damage or investigate source.
- Ceiling repair versus full ceiling refinish.

What changes price:

- Hole size.
- Texture difficulty.
- Ceiling versus wall.
- Height and access.
- Water damage.
- Whether insulation or framing is exposed.
- Paint blending requirements.

Common failures:

- Texture mismatch.
- Bad feathering under side light.
- Closing a wall before the leak is fixed.
- Not replacing compromised drywall after water exposure.
- Painting a patch with mismatched sheen.

Quote inputs:

- Photo from across the room.
- Angled closeup of texture.
- Measurement or object for scale.
- Leak source status if water was involved.

## Tile

Key homeowner decisions:

- Backsplash, floor, shower, fireplace, or accent wall.
- Ceramic, porcelain, stone, glass, mosaic, or large-format tile.
- Grout color and joint width.
- Waterproofing method for wet areas.
- Edge trim style.

What changes price:

- Tile size.
- Pattern complexity.
- Substrate condition.
- Demolition.
- Waterproofing.
- Leveling or crack isolation.
- Number of outlets, niches, corners, and transitions.

Common failures:

- Poor substrate prep.
- No waterproofing behind shower tile.
- Bad layout with tiny cuts at edges.
- Lippage on large-format tile.
- Cracked grout at movement joints.

Quote inputs:

- Surface photos.
- Tile box label or product link.
- Measurements.
- Wet or dry area.
- Current surface and demo needs.

## Flooring

Key homeowner decisions:

- LVP, engineered hardwood, solid hardwood, tile, laminate, or carpet replacement.
- Floating, glue-down, nail-down, or tile install.
- Baseboards removed and reset or shoe moulding added.
- Transition style.
- Whole level or room-by-room.

What changes price:

- Square footage.
- Demo type.
- Subfloor flatness.
- Stairs.
- Furniture.
- Transitions.
- Moisture mitigation.

Common failures:

- Not flattening slab before LVP.
- Tight expansion gaps.
- Bad door transitions.
- Stair nosing mismatch.
- Installing over moisture problems.

Quote inputs:

- Existing flooring type.
- Product specs.
- Room dimensions.
- Photos of transitions, stairs, and baseboards.

## Kitchen

Key homeowner decisions:

- Refresh, semi-remodel, or full remodel.
- Keep layout or move plumbing/electrical.
- Refinish, reface, or replace cabinets.
- Countertop material.
- Appliance sizes.
- Temporary kitchen plan.

What changes price:

- Cabinet scope.
- Wall removal.
- Electrical and lighting.
- Plumbing moves.
- Countertop material.
- Backsplash.
- Flooring.
- Permits and trade coordination.

Common failures:

- Choosing finishes before layout.
- Starting demo before cabinets are ready.
- Underplanning appliance specs.
- Not planning landing space.
- Ignoring daily living during remodel.

Quote inputs:

- Photos from each corner.
- Rough dimensions.
- Appliance specs.
- Must-have list.
- Budget comfort range.

## Bathroom

Key homeowner decisions:

- Tub-to-shower, vanity refresh, floor tile, or full gut.
- Waterproofing system.
- Tile size and layout.
- Shower glass style.
- Ventilation.
- Alternate bathroom access.

What changes price:

- Wet-area demolition.
- Tile selection.
- Plumbing changes.
- Waterproofing and substrate.
- Glass.
- Vanity and electrical.
- Hidden moisture damage.

Common failures:

- Treating tile as waterproofing.
- No slope at curb or niche.
- Bad fan sizing.
- Reusing compromised substrate.
- Poor glass blocking.

Quote inputs:

- Current bath photos.
- Desired layout.
- Tile and vanity direction.
- Any staining, softness, or recurring mildew.
