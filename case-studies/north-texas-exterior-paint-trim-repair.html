Exterior Painting - North Texas

North Texas exterior paint and trim repair built for sun, storms, and HOA review.

A North Texas exterior paint case study covering prep, trim repair, caulk failure, sun exposure, HOA color approval, primer, and weather scheduling.

CityNorth Texas
Timing3-7 day weather window
Budget lensExterior protection planning
The issue

What made this scope worth planning carefully.

Exterior paint in North Texas is a protection system. UV, hail, wind-driven rain, failed caulk, and trim movement expose weak prep quickly.

The plan

How ELF would sequence the work.

Wash, scrape, repair trim, replace failed caulk, spot-prime bare areas, confirm HOA colors, and schedule around wind, rain, and direct heat.

The finish

The standard that makes the project feel complete.

The best exterior finish is restrained: clean trim lines, durable coating, sealed joints, and color choices that protect resale and satisfy HOA requirements.

Scope checklist

  • Exterior inspection
  • Wash and scrape prep
  • Trim repair and caulk replacement
  • Primer and coating plan
  • Final walkaround

Quote signals

  • Do not paint over rotten trim
  • Watch direct-sun drying windows
  • Get HOA approval before ordering color
  • Use premium exterior coating on exposed elevations
Outcome

The repaint lasts longer because the scope is built around prep and protection rather than simply changing the color.
