What matters most in Allen.

A good Allen bathroom remodel starts with layout discipline. Many homes already have a workable footprint, so the question is whether to improve finishes, rework the shower, or rebuild the entire wet area. Keeping plumbing locations usually protects the budget; changing tub, toilet, or drain positions may be worth it only when the current plan is genuinely awkward.

ELF focuses on waterproofing, clean transitions, durable tile layouts, fan performance, and the kind of storage that keeps the room useful after the photos are done. In Allen, we pay special attention to hard-water marks, shower glass maintenance, builder mirror removal, vanity height, and whether the existing exhaust fan actually clears humidity.

Before you book.

A short, practical checklist for homeowners who want a cleaner estimate and fewer surprises.

Quick prep: send clear photos, rough measurements, the city, timeline, and what bothers you most. That turns the first conversation from vague pricing into a real scope discussion.
  • Measure shower width, vanity width, mirror width, and ceiling height before shopping fixtures.
  • Decide early whether you want to keep, remove, or reduce the tub.
  • Choose grout color with hard-water maintenance in mind, not only the tile sample.
  • Upgrade the exhaust fan if mirrors stay fogged for more than a few minutes.
  • Do not install new tile over soft, swollen, or water-stained wall board.

Local FAQs.

Short answers to the questions homeowners usually ask before scheduling bathroom remodel work in Allen.

How much does a bathroom remodel cost in Allen?

Many Allen bathroom projects land between $8,000 and $28,000 depending on shower scope, tile, vanity, plumbing changes, and whether hidden water damage is present. Small refreshes can be less; custom primary suites can be higher.

Is a tub-to-shower conversion worth it in Allen?

It is often worth it when the tub is rarely used and the shower is cramped. If resale is a concern, keeping at least one bathtub somewhere in the home is usually the better balance.

Can you update just the shower?

Yes. A shower-only remodel can make sense when the vanity, floor, and paint are still in good condition, but transitions must be planned so the room does not look patched together.
