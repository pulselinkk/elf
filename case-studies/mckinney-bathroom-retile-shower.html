Bathroom Retile - McKinney, TX

McKinney bathroom retile focused on waterproofing, clean lines, and daily durability.

A McKinney bathroom retile case study explaining waterproofing decisions, tile layout, grout choice, shower hardware, ventilation, and finish sequencing.

CityMcKinney, TX
Timing2-4 week planning window
Budget lensTile and shower scope planning
The issue

What made this scope worth planning carefully.

The visible tile is only one part of the job. McKinney bath projects need waterproofing, substrate checks, drain planning, ventilation review, and clear decisions about what stays versus what gets rebuilt.

The plan

How ELF would sequence the work.

Start with water management, then tile layout. Confirm niches, glass, plumbing trim, wall flatness, and grout type before ordering material so the finished shower looks intentional from every angle.

The finish

The standard that makes the project feel complete.

Large clean tile, tighter grout lines where appropriate, a properly sloped shower floor, sealed transitions, and simple hardware make the room easier to clean and better for resale.

Scope checklist

  • Demo and substrate inspection
  • Waterproofing plan
  • Tile layout and cut planning
  • Grout, caulk, and transition detail
  • Vanity, mirror, and paint coordination

Quote signals

  • Do not tile over a moisture problem
  • Plan niches before waterproofing
  • Use the right grout for wet areas
  • Check ventilation before closing walls

Visible transformation, scoped with practical constraints.

Outcome

The project reads as finished rather than patched because waterproofing, tile layout, fixture placement, and paint touch-ups are treated as one system.
