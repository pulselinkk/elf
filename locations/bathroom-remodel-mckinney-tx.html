McKinney bathroom reality

What we find in McKinney homes.

McKinney has a wide range of housing stock — from 1980s-90s ranch homes in Eldorado to early-2000s builds in Stonebridge Ranch to newer construction in Craig Ranch (2008-2020). Each era has its own bathroom problem profile.

In the older stock (pre-2000): fiberglass tub surrounds deteriorating at the seams, builder-grade toilets with float valve issues, single-pane mirrors over basic vanity strips, pink or avocado tile that seemed like a good idea at the time. In the 2000s-era homes: cultured marble vanity tops cracking at the edges, jetted tubs that nobody uses and everyone wants to convert, 12x12 beige travertine with grout issues. In Craig Ranch new construction: generally in good shape, but aging fixtures and grout that needs re-sealing.

Most popular upgrade

The tub-to-shower conversion.

By far our most requested McKinney bathroom project: removing the jetted or standard tub in the master bath and replacing it with a curbless or low-threshold walk-in shower. This typically involves: full demolition of the tub and surround, Schluter KERDI waterproofing membrane, new drain (linear or center), 12x24 or 24x48 porcelain tile floor and walls, frameless glass enclosure, and a comfort-height toilet while we're in there.

McKinney Building Inspections requires a permit for plumbing drain changes — we pull that permit and schedule the rough and final inspections. Typical turnaround from permit approval to completion is 12-16 days.

Neighborhoods served

McKinney areas we're in every week.

Craig Ranch (75070): High demand for master bath upgrades and accessibility modifications. Lots of aging-in-place projects.
Stonebridge Ranch (75070): Mixed housing age — early builds need full gut-outs, newer sections want cosmetic upgrades.
Eldorado (75069): Older stock, more structural surprises behind walls. We factor that into our estimates honestly.
Westridge and Tucker Hill: Newer construction, usually cosmetic scopes.
Trinity Falls: Mix of new construction and 5-10 year old homes, mostly maintenance and upgrade work.

Secondary baths typically run 40-50% less than master bath projects of the same scope.
