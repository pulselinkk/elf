Texas deck refinishing is really UV management.

Ranges assume common North Texas wood decks. Composite cleaning, heavy railing detail, multi-level decks, and structural repairs can change the scope.

Decks in Texas take a beating from heat, sun, sudden storms, and seasonal movement. A deck that looks gray or patchy is not just a color problem; the wood surface may be drying, checking, or losing protection.

The cost depends on whether the deck needs a simple wash and recoat, a full strip and sand, board replacement, railing work, or structural repairs before finish.

This guide helps you choose between maintenance staining, restoration, and replacement conversations.

Field note: Best budget move: recoat before the finish fully fails. Waiting until stain peels or wood fibers break down turns maintenance into restoration.

What moves the price.

The deck surface tells you whether you are maintaining, restoring, or rebuilding.

Condition

Gray wood is not all the same.

Surface gray can be cleaned. Deep checking, splinters, rot, and cupping need more work before stain.

Inspect before pricing
Old coating

Peeling stain costs more.

A failing film has to be removed or stabilized. Staining over peeling finish wastes money.

Prep is the project
Stain type

Solid and semi-transparent behave differently.

Solid hides flaws and blocks UV better. Semi-transparent shows grain and is easier to maintain when wood is healthy.

Pick for condition
Weather

Timing matters in Texas.

Stain needs dry wood and the right temperature window. Rain, heat, and humidity can delay the job.

Schedule with patience
Railings

Rails are labor-heavy.

Balusters, posts, stairs, and edges take more time than open deck boards.

Linear feet matter
Safety

Finish will not fix structure.

Loose stairs, soft boards, unstable railings, and rot must be repaired before cosmetics.

Texas deck notes.

Local conditions, buyer expectations, climate, and typical construction methods change how a project should be planned in North Texas.

Local planning notes
  • Full-sun decks in DFW may need maintenance closer to every 18-24 months. Shaded decks can have more mildew issues instead.
  • Spring and fall are better stain windows than peak summer afternoons.
  • Cedar looks beautiful with semi-transparent stain when maintained. Severely weathered cedar may need solid stain or board replacement.
  • Hail and storm seasons can create small surface damage that becomes water entry over time.
Before you approve a quote

Ask whether the proposal includes protection, material handling, disposal, prep, finish details, and cleanup. The missing line items are usually where budgets get messy.

Good rule

If two quotes are far apart, compare scope line by line before assuming one contractor is simply cheaper.

How the work should unfold.

A clean sequence protects the home, reduces rework, and keeps decisions from arriving too late.

Inspect and test

Check boards, fasteners, railing stability, old coating, moisture, and sun exposure.

Clean or strip

Wash, strip if needed, neutralize, rinse, and allow proper dry time.

Repair and sand

Replace damaged boards, set fasteners, sand rough fibers, and prep railings.

Stain and cure

Apply the finish in correct weather, protect drying time, and give maintenance guidance.

Deck refinishing checklist.

Use this before requesting a quote or buying materials. It keeps the conversation concrete.

Checklist
  • Check for soft boards before staining.
  • Confirm whether old stain is peeling or only faded.
  • Choose solid or semi-transparent stain based on wood condition.
  • Plan around rain and sprinkler schedules.
  • Move furniture and grills before the crew arrives.
  • Schedule maintenance before the finish fails completely.
Do not skip
  • Do not stain damp wood.
  • Do not coat over peeling stain.
  • Do not ignore loose railings or stair movement.
  • Do not use interior-grade products outside.
Estimate tip

Ask for the finish standard.

Every quote should define what counts as complete: cleanup, touch-ups, paint blending, material disposal, and what happens if hidden damage appears.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers to the questions homeowners ask before they commit to the scope.

How often should a Texas deck be refinished?

Many decks need attention every 18 to 36 months depending on sun, shade, product type, foot traffic, and how exposed the deck is.

Is solid stain better than semi-transparent?

Solid stain hides more and blocks UV well, but can peel if maintenance is delayed. Semi-transparent shows wood grain and is easier to maintain when the wood is healthy.

Can a gray deck be saved?

Often yes if the wood is structurally sound. Cleaning, sanding, and the right stain can restore many gray decks. Rot, deep splitting, or unsafe framing changes the conversation.

What weather is best for staining?

Mild, dry weather with no rain in the forecast and wood that has dried properly after washing. Extreme heat can make application harder.
