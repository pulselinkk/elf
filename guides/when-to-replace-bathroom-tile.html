Start with the real decision.

Bathroom tile can be ugly and still functional, or beautiful and failing behind the wall. This guide shows when repair, regrout, retile, or full waterproofing work makes sense.

Tile is the finish layer, not the entire bathroom system. In showers, waterproofing behind tile matters more than grout color. On floors, substrate movement and slab conditions decide whether cracks return.

Before replacing bathroom tile for appearance, confirm whether there are water, ventilation, or substrate issues that should be solved during the project.

Field note: If the same issue has returned more than once, solve the cause before spending money on the finish.

North Texas notes.

DFW homes have patterns shaped by heat, storms, clay soil, slab construction, hard water, and buyer expectations.

Local planning notes
  • DFW hard water can make shower tile look older than it is.
  • Slab movement can crack bathroom floor tile if substrate planning is weak.
  • Primary bathrooms in Southlake and Frisco often justify higher finish quality for resale.
Slow down when needed

Active water, unsafe access, structural symptoms, electrical risk, gas, refrigerant, or hidden waterproofing should be addressed before cosmetic work.

Homeowner checklist.

Use this before buying materials, starting DIY work, or asking for an estimate.

Checklist
  • Tap tiles gently and listen for hollow spots.
  • Check corners for failed caulk.
  • Look for swelling at baseboards or vanity sides.
  • Run the fan and confirm airflow.
  • Save spare tile after any project.
Estimate tip

Send context, not only close-ups.

Include one wide photo, one close-up, rough measurements, the city, when the issue started, and what has already been tried.

Decision rule

Protect the expensive layer.

When water, structure, substrate, code, or finish quality is involved, solve that layer before spending on visible finishes.

Frequently asked questions.

Short answers for deciding whether this is DIY, maintenance, repair, or a professional project.

Can cracked bathroom tile be repaired?

One cracked tile can often be replaced if the substrate is stable and matching tile exists.

Does regrouting fix shower leaks?

Not if the waterproofing or backing has failed. Grout is not the waterproof layer.

Should I replace tile before selling?

Only if it is damaged, badly dated for the price point, or making the bathroom feel poorly maintained.

What matters most in a shower retile?

Waterproofing, slope, substrate, layout, and ventilation matter before tile style.
